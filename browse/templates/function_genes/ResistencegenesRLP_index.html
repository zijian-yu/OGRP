Resistence genes

Plant disease resistance genes (R-genes) play a key role in recognizing proteins expressed by specific avirulence (Avr) genes of pathogens. R-genes originate from a phylogenetically ancient form of immunity that is common to plants and animals. However, the rapid evolution of plant immunity systems has led to enormous gene diversification. Although little is known about these agriculturally important genes, some fundamental genomic features have already been described.
